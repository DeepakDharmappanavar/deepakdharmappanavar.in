About Me

I’m a software engineer who enjoys building systems that survive real users, real scale, and real constraints. I work mostly on backend and cloud infrastructure, where clean abstractions, observability, and reliability matter more than buzzwords.

My journey has been shaped by competitive programming, distributed systems, and hands-on cloud engineering. I like problems that start messy and end simple. Performance issues, scaling bottlenecks, and design trade-offs are the kind of puzzles I willingly lose sleep over.

Alongside building, I care deeply about teaching and community. As a Ex-GDG On-Campus Lead and a Gold Microsoft Learn Student Ambassador, I’ve mentored thousands of students through workshops, labs, and developer programs, helping them move from tutorials to tangible systems.

When I’m not coding, I’m usually thinking about system design, developer tooling, or how to explain complex ideas clearly. If you’re building something interesting or just want to talk tech over coffee, I’m always happy to connect.
